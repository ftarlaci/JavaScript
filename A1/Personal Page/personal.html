Fatma Tarlaci

Fatma earned her Ph.D. in Comparative Literature from the University of Texas at Austin in 2016. She was a tecahing assistant and graduate student assistant instructor at UT Austin throughout her doctoral studies. During her tenure, she taught various courses in writing and rhetoric, literature, and research to undergraduate students. Immediately after, she transitioned into a new field and program in computer science at Stanford University, specializing in computer science education. She will graduate from her current program at the end of Summer 2018 and is planning to teach in higher education in the field of computer science.

After joining her graduate program at Stanford, Fatma studied various topics in computer science, such as diversity in cs departments. Some of her research interests include improving retaining of diver students and working on curriculum development regarding introductory computer science programming courses.
